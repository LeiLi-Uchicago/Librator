Librator: effcient and effective protein expression by using Gibson Cloning

Artificial mutagenesis is the predominant way to investigate the impact of mutations on a variety of phenotypes, including antigenicity, virulence and binding affinity. Chimeric protein design is a promising way to achieve a successful universal vaccine design. Both methods require abundant and accurate sequence editing and protein expression, which pose great challenges to both biologists and resources. Expensive reagent costs for protein expression, complicated HA numbering systems and fallible sequence editing methods highly limit the breadth, efficiency and accuracy of related research. To overcome these challenges, we propose a tool called “Librator” to help biologists express and design their proteins more economically, accurately and efficiently. By introducing Gibson Clone Assembly, “Librator” allows biologists split their sequences into a few standardized fragments, allowing for significant reagent savings by reusing highly conserved fragments shared by multiple sequences. The graphical user interface and HA numbering alignment system help biologists easily locate interesting residues using any of three HA numbering systems. Furthermore, the integrated sequence mutate/edit functions allow users to generate mutations and chimeric proteins efficiently and accurately. In conclusion, “Librator” is committed to liberating biologists from limited resource and time-intensive, repetitive and fallible sequence editing, so that they can extend the breadth of their research and focus more on biological design and research, which need more original and critical thinking. Librator is an open-source tool available on a public repository.
